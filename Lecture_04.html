Physical Properties of the NMR experiment


• If you have a magnetic field and place a magnet in it, there are two possibilities:
◇ Paramagnetic: The magnet enhances the field
◇ Diamagnetic: The magnet decreases the field
• The higher the current, the stronger the magnetic moment
• In NMR we measure the sum of all the spins and no single spin
• If there is no phase coherance all magnetization in the xy-plane cancel each other out
• The z-magnetization follows a boltzmann distribution for the quotient of the beta to alpha state
◇ The differences are very small: 1000 to 1001 so the z-magnetization is not 0 but very small
◇ We are insensitive to this in NMR since we look in the xy plane
• In the middle of the spectrum the frequency is 0 and from that we measure relative frequencies

How does the detector work


• After taking up the signal it is splitted
◇ One of two is 90° shifted and sin modulated
◇ The other one is cos modulated
• Then in a mixer we feed in the frequency which should be in the middle
◇ The center frequency is then substracted from all frequencies
• The frequencies are mixed and amplified
• This relative measurement assures that we measure the same ppm on all the spectrometers independent of field strength

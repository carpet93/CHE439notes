Physical Properties of the NMR experiment


• If you have a magnetic field and place a magnet in it, there are two possibilities:
◇ Paramagnetic: The magnet enhances the field
◇ Diamagnetic: The magnet decreases the field
• The higher the current, the stronger the magnetic moment
• In NMR we measure the sum of all the spins and no single spin
• If there is no phase coherance all magnetization in the xy-plane cancel each other out
• The z-magnetization follows a boltzmann distribution for the quotient of the beta to alpha state
◇ The differences are very small: 1000 to 1001 so the z-magnetization is not 0 but very small
◇ We are insensitive to this in NMR since we look in the xy plane
• In the middle of the spectrum the frequency is 0 and from that we measure relative frequencies

How does the detector work

?
• After taking up the signal it is splitted
◇ One of two is 90° shifted and sin modulated
◇ The other one is cos modulated
• Then in a mixer we feed in the frequency which should be in the middle
◇ The center frequency is then substracted from all frequencies
• The frequencies are mixed and amplified
• This relative measurement assures that we measure the same ppm on all the spectrometers independent of field strength

How far do we measure and what can we excite?
• The spin in the rotating frame is static when its frequency is exactly the frequency of the field
◇ So if we have 5 ppm on a 600 MHz, the spin is rotating in the xy plane with 3000 Hz compared to the B1 field which is rotating in the xy-plane
• If I rotate at 1H frequencies i will never change carbon spins because their frequencies are so far off that the B1 field doesnt matter
◇ The B1 frequency can be controlled by us (setting the center freqeuncy)
◇ If the spin oscilates with the same frequency we define as center frequency, it will occur in the center of the spectrum because it is always in the same orientation as the B1 field
• The further away i move from the center frequency, the higher the z-field gets
◇ If we are very far away, only the z-field matters and the spin is not rotated anymore → not visible in NMR (e.g. if we use 1H frequencies we cant rotate 13C spins)
• The pulse only affects spins, that are close to the center frequency
◇ How close they need to be is defined by the strength of the center frequency.
• If B1 field gets very weak, you can only excite a single line from a multiplet
• The power in Watts of the B1 field defines how far we get signals
